# Skill: On-Page SEO for NLP

## 🎯 Objective
Traditional SEO focuses on placing keywords in strategic locations (titles, headings, throughout the text). NLP SEO goes a step further by treating content optimization as "technical SEO for content understanding." This skill modifies sentence structure, formatting, and word proximity to ensure search engine parsers clearly understand the relationships, entities, and answers on a web page.

## 🧠 Core NLP Concepts to Acknowledge
1. **Tokenization:** Breaking a sentence into its distinct terms.
2. **Parts-of-Speech Tagging:** Classifying words by their grammatical roles (nouns, verbs, adjectives).
3. **Lemmatization:** Recognizing that different forms of a word share the same root.
4. **Word Dependency:** Creating relationships between words based on grammatical rules.
5. **Named Entity Extraction:** Identifying words with a "known" factual meaning (people, places, concepts).

## 🛠️ Execution Steps

### Step 1: Optimize the "Units of Understanding"
*   **Sentence Level:** Keep sentences concise and grammatically straightforward. Reduce "dependency hops" between subject and object.
*   **Paragraph Level:** Limit paragraphs to 3-4 sentences focusing on one micro-topic.
*   **Section Level:** Group related paragraphs effectively under descriptive headings.
*   **Page Level:** Ensure consistent representation of the macro-topic.

### Step 2: Use Structure to Convey Semantic Meaning
*   **The Inverted Pyramid:** Place vital information, answers, and entities at the very top of the page.
*   **Heading Boundaries:** Treat headings as boundaries defining the context of the text beneath.
*   **Manage Proximity:** Keep highly relevant entities within the same sentence.

### Step 3: Satisfy "Expected Units and Classifications"
*   *Rule:* Identify the expected unit of your target query (currency, date, measurement).
*   *Example:* Temperature must have a numerical value and "degrees".

## 📋 Processing Routine
1. Analyze for long, complex sentences and break them into shorter, declarative statements.
2. Move entities closer to improve proximity.
3. Evaluate hierarchy (H1, H2, H3).
4. Check for mandatory units (USD, KES, months, years).
