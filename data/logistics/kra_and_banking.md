# KRA PIN & Banking for Foreigners

## 1. Non-Resident KRA PIN
A KRA (Kenya Revenue Authority) PIN is essential for:
- Opening bank accounts.
- Buying a car or motorbike.
- Engaging in business.

### Requirements
- **Passport Copy**: Valid for 6 months.
- **Introduction Letter**: From a tax representative or a bank.
- **Contact Details**: A Kenyan phone number (Safaricom recommended).
- **Physical Address**: Even a temporary one (e.g., Airbnb in Westlands/Diani).

### Process
- Applied via the **iTax Portal** (https://itax.kra.go.ke).
- VizaBot KE automates the data entry preparation for this step.

## 2. Recommended Banks (USD Friendly)
Foreigners often need USD or Multi-currency accounts to avoid KES volatility.

### I&M Bank
- **Pros**: Excellent online banking, easy USD sub-accounts, widespread ATMs.
- **Min Balance**: Ranges from $100 - $500 depending on the account tier.

### NCBA Bank
- **Pros**: Strong digital platform, integrated with M-Pesa (via M-Shwari), reliable for international wire transfers (SWIFT).

### Standard Chartered
- **Pros**: Premium service, "Expat" account options, high security.
- **Cons**: Stricter documentation requirements.

---

## 3. M-Pesa (Mobile Money)
- **Essential**: 99% of transactions in Kenya (including government fees) use M-Pesa.
- **Activation**: Requires a Safaricom SIM card and a valid Passport.
- **Tip**: Nomads should register for **M-Pesa Global** to receive funds from abroad easily.
